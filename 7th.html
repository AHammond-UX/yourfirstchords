Dominant 7th Chords

Introduction

Dominant 7th chords are a great next step once you’re comfortable with basic major and minor shapes. They’re formed by taking a major chord and adding one extra note called the minor 7th. This small change creates a sound that’s bluesy, slightly tense, and full of character. You’ll hear dominant 7th chords in blues, rock, jazz, and many classic guitar riffs.

A7

Chord tones: A (root), C♯ (major third), E (perfect fifth), G (minor seventh)

D7

Chord tones: D (root), F♯ (major third), A (perfect fifth), C (minor seventh)

G7

Chord tones: G (root), B (major third), D (perfect fifth), F (minor seventh)

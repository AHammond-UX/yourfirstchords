Minor Chords

Introduction

Minor chords have a darker, more emotional sound compared to major chords. They’re created by changing just one note in a major chord: the major third becomes a minor third. This small change gives minor chords their sad, moody character. In this section, you’ll learn the essential open-position minor chord shapes.

A Minor

Chord tones: A (root), C (minor third), E (perfect fifth)

D Minor

Chord tones: D (root), F (minor third), A (perfect fifth)

E Minor

Chord tones: E (root), G (minor third), B (perfect fifth)

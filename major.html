Major Chords

Introduction

Major chords are some of the first shapes beginners learn because they sound bright, happy, and full. A major chord is built from three notes: the root, the major third, and the perfect fifth. On this page, you’ll learn the most common open-position major chords and the notes that make each one up.

A Major

Chord tones: A (root), C♯ (major third), E (perfect fifth)

C Major

Chord tones: C (root), E (major third), G (perfect fifth)

D Major

Chord tones: D (root), F♯ (major third), A (perfect fifth)

E Major

Chord tones: E (root), G♯ (major third), B (perfect fifth)

F Major

Chord tones: F (root), A (major third), C (perfect fifth)

G Major

Chord tones: G (root), B (major third), D (perfect fifth)
